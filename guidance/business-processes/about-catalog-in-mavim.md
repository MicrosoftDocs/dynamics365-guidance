# Introduction to the Microsoft business process catalog in Mavim 

The Microsoft business process catalog provides a best‑practice foundation for designing and executing a Dynamics 365 implementation lifecycle. When integrated into Mavim, this catalog becomes an operational blueprint that aligns strategy, processes, technology, and people, setting the stage for predictable, scalable, and first‑time/ongoing right transformation.

This article explains:

- What the business process catalog (BPC) is and how it is structured in Mavim
- How Mavim enhances the BPC with process management, AI, process mining, and design capabilities
- What the difference is between Mavim and Azure DevOps for transformation 
- How this combination supports Success by Design
- How to get started with multiple onboarding pathways for the business process catalog in Mavim
## What is the Microsoft business process catalog?

The business process catalog is Microsoft’s standardized library of how organizations should optimally operate on Dynamics 365. It serves as a best‑practice, research‑driven framework built from:

- Learnings from Microsoft FastTrack
- Global partner experience
- Real‑world customer implementations
- Industry-standard process modeling practices

To learn more about the business process catalog, see [About the Business Process Catalog.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/guidance/business-processes/about)

### Catalog structure

The BPC is built in four hierarchical levels:

1. 15 industry‑standard end‑to‑end processes
2. Process areas within each end‑to‑end process
3. 600+ unique business processes
4. 3000+ detailed scenarios
This hierarchy ensures full visibility from enterprise‑wide value streams down to step-level execution.

To learn more about the levels in the BPC, see [Overview of the Business Process Catalog Levels.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/guidance/business-processes/about-catalog-levels)

### Why the business process catalog matters

The catalog enables organizations to:

- Start from standardized “to‑be” processes
- Reduce time spent defining business requirements
- Align business and IT early in the project
- Identify gaps and risks before design begins
- Create a shared language between teams and partners
When brought into Mavim, this becomes not just reference content, but a living, navigable, visual process map for your whole journey.

## How Mavim enhances the business process catalog

The Mavim AI Business Process Transformation Suite turns the BPC into an interactive, collaborative operating model. Mavim provides:

### 1. Business process management

A centralized, integrated repository for all enterprise processes.

- Full process hierarchy navigation
- RACI, KPIs, owners, and metadata
- Versioning and governance
- Collaboration between customer and partner teams
  
### 2. Process mining

AI‑assisted discovery of how processes actually run inside Dynamics.

- Telemetry analysis
- Deviation detection
- Bottleneck identification
- Exception clustering

This helps to close the loop between intended and real behavior.

### 3. Continuous improvement

With BPM + mining combined:

- Compare actual vs. target processes
- Track maturity
- Analyze hotspots
- Prioritize improvement initiatives
- Build data‑driven business cases

### 4. Solution blueprint design

Unique to Mavim is the ability to:

- Map processes directly to Dynamics 365 functionality
- Connect to Azure DevOps for requirement and project management
- Synchronize process IDs with Azure DevOps work items
- Trace change impact across processes, roles, and applications

This produces a “living blueprint” forever, not a static document and allows you to further optimize perpetually. 

### AI readiness for business process transformation

AI readiness ensures organizations can effectively leverage AI‑driven insights, automation, and predictions across the Dynamics 365 and Mavim transformation lifecycle. Key readiness pillars include data quality and governance, process standardization, clear ownership structures, and the ability to measure performance with telemetry. When the business process catalog is managed inside Mavim, organizations establish the foundation required for scalable AI adoption and agentic implementation ensuring models are trained on accurate, well‑defined, and continuously improved processes.

## Difference between Mavim and Azure DevOps in a Dynamics 365 transformation

Mavim and Azure DevOps work together during a Dynamics 365 implementation, but they solve different problems and serve different audiences.

### Mavim — The business process and transformation layer

Mavim is where the business definition of the transformation lives. It manages:

- Process modeling and visualization
- Best-practice adoption using the Business Process Catalog
- Process mining and operational telemetry
- Continuous improvement cycles
- Solution blueprint design
- Role and responsibility mapping
- Traceability of business impact
- Business–IT alignment across the lifecycle

Mavim answers: “How should the business operate, and how does Dynamics support that?”

### Azure DevOps — The delivery execution and engineering layer

Azure DevOps is where technical implementation work is executed and tracked. It manages:

- Product backlogs
- User stories, tasks, and acceptance criteria
- Sprints, workflows, and burndown
- Test plans and results
- Deployment pipelines and code repositories
- DevOps automation

Azure DevOps answers: “What needs to be built, tested, and delivered to make the solution real?”

### How the two work together

- Mavim provides the business process context and maps it to Dynamics capabilities.
- DevOps provides the execution engine that turns those mapped processes into working solution components.
- Mavim synchronizes process IDs and requirements into DevOps, ensuring end‑to‑end traceability from process → requirement → configuration → test → deployment.

In essence:

- Mavim defines and optimizes the business blueprint.
- DevOps executes and delivers it.

To learn more about how Mavim and Azure DevOps work together to ensure a successful Dynamics implementation see, [Mavim and Azure DevOps integration](about-azure-devops-mavim-integration.md).

## Get started with the business process catalog in Mavim pathways

To align with Success by Design and FastTrack recommended practices, you can choose the best onboarding paths for your organization depending on where you are in your Dynamics transformation.

### 1. Start from the business process catalog inside Mavim

- Download the Microsoft Business Process Catalog Mavim database and import into your Mavim environment. Download the catalog [https://aka.ms/businessprocesscatalog.](https://aka.ms/businessprocesscatalog) 
- Review end‑to‑end processes
- Assign process owners
- Synchronize your Mavim environment to your Azure DevOps environment
Best for organizations starting a new ERP or CRM journey.

### 2. Map and benchmark your own processes

- Import existing process documentation to Mavim
- Map your custom processes to the standard processes in the catalog and benchmark against BPC best practices
- Identify gaps and improvement opportunities
Best for organizations modernizing existing Dynamics implementations.

### 3. Start with Process Mining 

- Connect Mavim process mining to Dynamics 365
- Discover real process flows
- Use AI‑detected patterns to prioritize improvements
Best for organizations with operational inefficiencies that are already live on Dynamics 365.
