# Onboard and manage the employee lifecycle

***Applies to: Dynamics 365 Customer Voice, Dynamics 365 Field Service, Dynamics 365 Finance, Dynamics 365 Guides, Dynamics 365 Human Resources, Dynamics 365 Remote Assist, Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management***

This article describes the end-to-end business process to streamline employee onboarding and manage the employee lifecycle, ultimately enhancing overall workforce engagement.

In today's competitive business landscape, retaining and developing top talent is crucial for organizational success. Onboarding and managing employee lifecycles are a strategic approach to secure a positive and productive employee experience. Effective onboarding and employee lifecycle management foster a cohesive and supportive work environment that empowers employees to contribute their best. Dynamics 365 provides a comprehensive suite of tools to streamline and enhance various aspects of onboarding and employee lifecycle management, from creating customized onboarding checklists and managing employee records to administering compensation and benefits, tracking performance and growth, managing leave plans and attendance, and reconciling travel expenses.

Onboarding and managing employee lifecycle processes serves two purposes in any organization. Firstly, they ensure the smooth transition of new hires into the organization, fostering a positive and productive work environment. Secondly, they help organizations comply with labor laws and regulations, minimize legal risks, and build a reputation as an employer of choice. Dynamics 365 implementations streamline and enhance these processes, providing a centralized platform for managing employee data, records, and communication.

A key advantage of incorporating onboarding and employee lifecycle management processes within Dynamics 365 lies in their adaptability. These processes aren't rigidly prescribed, empowering organizations to customize their employee management approach to suit their unique needs and timelines. They can be seamlessly integrated into the system at any stage of its implementation or gradually added as the organization progresses. This adaptability ensures that businesses can swiftly adapt to evolving business requirements, growth, and industry trends without disrupting their operations. As a result, Dynamics 365 proves to be an invaluable tool for long-term employee management and engagement.

## Stakeholders

Many people across the organization should contribute to the decision-making process and design of onboard and manage employee lifecycle area. The following list provides examples of such stakeholders:

- **Human resources assistants** play a crucial role in ensuring a smooth onboarding experience for new hires. They manage the administrative tasks involved in onboarding.

- **Line managers** are directly responsible for the guidance and supervision of new employees. They provide mentorship, facilitate regular performance reviews, and identify training and development opportunities to enhance employee skills and knowledge.

- **HR analysts** bring insights that are instrumental in optimizing the onboarding process, ensuring that new hires receive a smooth and effective integration into the organization. HR analysts can identify areas for improvement in onboarding materials, training programs, and mentorship opportunities.

- **Onboarding coordinators** are responsible for designing and implementing comprehensive onboarding programs that effectively integrate new hires into the organization. They develop and manage onboarding materials, including handbooks, orientation materials, and training modules.

- **Performance management specialists** play a critical role in aligning performance management systems with company goals and employee career aspirations. They develop and implement performance management processes that foster transparency, accountability, and professional development opportunities.

- **Compensation and benefits specialists** oversee the organization's compensation and benefits programs, ensuring fairness, competitiveness, and regulatory compliance. They provide guidance on benefits enrollment, payroll, and related administration.

- **Training and development specialists** play a vital role in designing, developing, and delivering training and development programs that align with employee needs and organizational objectives.

Each of these stakeholders contribute with specialized expertise to the successful onboarding and management of the employee lifecycle. Contributions between stakeholders foster a smooth and engaging onboarding experience, promote employee development, and enable organizations to achieve their talent management objectives.

## Onboard and manage employee lifecycle benefits

There are many key benefits that can be used to monitor and measure the success of implementing technology to support the *Onboard and manage employee lifecycle* benefits. The following sections outline the key benefits that an organization might monitor and measure for *Onboard and manage employee lifecycle* benefits.

### Optimize the onboarding process and improve employee engagement

Dynamics 365 provides a centralized platform for managing the onboarding process, ensuring that new hires receive a smooth and consistent experience. With customizable onboarding templates and checklists, HR teams can efficiently guide new employees through the process, from paperwork and benefits enrollment to training and orientation Interactive onboarding portals can be created with Power Apps or Power Pages. Automated workflows using Power Automate can trigger tasks and notifications, ensuring that onboarding activities are completed promptly.

### Empower managers with onboarding tools

Dynamics 365 empowers managers to actively participate in the onboarding process, providing them with a centralized platform to manage tasks, track progress, and provide feedback. Managers can identify potential issues and provide timely support, ensuring a smooth and successful transition into their roles.

### Continuously improve onboarding practices

By analyzing onboarding data, HR teams can identify trends and patterns that inform continuous improvement efforts. This data-driven approach enables organizations to adapt their onboarding strategies to the needs of their workforce, ensuring that new hires receive the support and resources they need to thrive in their new roles.
